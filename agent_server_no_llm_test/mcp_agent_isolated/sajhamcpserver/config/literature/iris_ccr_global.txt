WHAT IS IRIS?
-------------
IRIS is the Internal CCR (Counterparty Credit Risk) limit and exposure management platform. It
provides a daily snapshot-based view of credit limits, current exposures, and available headroom
across all counterparties, products, and legal entities. Each row in the IRIS dataset represents
the state of one limit record as of a specific snapshot date.


WHAT IS COUNTERPARTY CREDIT RISK (CCR)?
----------------------------------------
CCR is the risk that a counterparty to a financial contract will default on its obligations before
the final settlement of the contract's cash flows. Unlike traditional lending risk, CCR applies
to bilateral derivative contracts (e.g., interest rate swaps, FX forwards) where the exposure
can move in either direction. CCR encompasses pre-settlement risk, replacement cost risk, and
potential future exposure (PFE).


HIERARCHY OF LIMIT LEVELS
--------------------------
IRIS tracks exposure and limits at three independent levels:

1. PRODUCT LEVEL
   - The most granular level. Controls exposure for a specific product type (e.g., FX Forward,
     Derivative Products, Interest Rate Products, Equity Products, Commodity Products) for a
     given counterparty under a specific legal entity and facility.
   - Columns: Product Limit, Product Exposure, Product Avail

2. CUSTOMER LEVEL
   - Aggregates exposure across all products for a given counterparty (Customer Code) under a
     specific legal entity. Represents the total credit relationship at the customer level.
   - Columns: Cust Limit, Cust Exposure, Cust Avail

3. CONNECTION LEVEL
   - The broadest level. Aggregates exposure across all legal entities and all products for a
     connection group (group of related entities sharing a Connection Code). Used for group-level
     credit control.
   - Columns: Conn Limit, Conn Exposure, Conn Avail


!!!!! CRITICAL NON-AGGREGATION RULE !!!!!
-----------------------------------------
LIMITS AT DIFFERENT LEVELS ARE PARALLEL, INDEPENDENT CONTROLS — NEVER SUM THEM TOGETHER.

Product Limit + Customer Limit + Connection Limit MUST NOT be added.

Each level represents a different risk dimension and control point. A transaction may be checked
against all three simultaneously, but the limits are not additive. Always report each level
separately with its own limit_key. Summing across levels would produce a meaningless and
misleading number.

Similarly, do NOT sum Product Limits across different facilities or legal entities for the same
counterparty — each limit_key is an independent control.


LIMITS vs EXPOSURE vs HEADROOM
-------------------------------
- LIMIT: The maximum permitted exposure for a given counterparty / product / legal entity.
  Established by credit officers and approved through the credit process.

- EXPOSURE: The current mark-to-market (MTM) or notional exposure on the date of the snapshot.
  This is the actual risk amount outstanding.

- HEADROOM (Avail): Headroom = Limit - Exposure. Positive headroom means capacity remains.
  Negative headroom means the limit is BREACHED (exposure exceeds the approved limit).

- BREACH: When Exposure > Limit (i.e., Avail is negative). Breaches require immediate escalation
  and reporting to credit risk officers.


COMPOSITE LIMIT KEY
--------------------
Each limit record is uniquely identified by a composite limit key:

    limit_key = CustomerCode / FacilityID / LegalEntity / Product

Example: "RBC / 76830 / BCMC / FX Forward"

This key identifies exactly which limit record you are looking at. Always use the limit_key when
reporting breach findings or limit details to avoid ambiguity.


UEN / FACILITY ID
------------------
The Facility ID (also called UEN — Unique Entity Number in some contexts) is a numeric identifier
assigned to each credit facility. It is specific to a counterparty-legal entity combination.
Multiple products can share the same Facility ID if they are booked under the same facility.


CSA AGREEMENT (Credit Support Annex)
--------------------------------------
A CSA (Credit Support Annex) is a legal document under the ISDA Master Agreement framework that
governs collateral posting between two counterparties. If CSA Agreement = Y, the counterparty
has a collateral agreement in place, which reduces net exposure by allowing margin calls.
If CSA Agreement = N, there is no collateral support — the full exposure is at risk.


NETTING AGREEMENT
-----------------
A Netting Agreement allows the firm to offset positive and negative mark-to-market values across
multiple contracts with the same counterparty. If Netting Agreement = Y, exposures may be netted,
which can significantly reduce the reported gross exposure. If Netting Agreement = N, exposures
are computed on a gross basis.


SNAPSHOT LOGIC
--------------
- The IRIS dataset is snapshot-based. Each row captures the state of a limit on a specific Date.
- Dates are stored in YYYY-MM-DD format.
- Always filter by Date before performing any analysis. The latest available date represents
  the most current view of the book.
- Trend analysis requires comparing the same limit_key across multiple snapshot dates.
- Never interpolate between dates — only compare actual snapshot values.


LEGAL ENTITIES
--------------
IRIS covers the following legal entities (booking entities):
  BCMC  - Primary booking entity (most common)
  BMO   - Bank of Montreal entity
  BNBI  - Secondary entity
  BOMI  - International entity
  HARRIS BANK - US entity
  BHIC  - Holding company entity
  BMN   - Nordic entity
  BCML  - Luxembourg entity
  BLAC  - Asset management entity
  BMMC  - Capital markets entity

A counterparty may have limits under multiple legal entities simultaneously. Each legal entity
is an independent booking entity with its own limits and exposures.


SCALE AND PERFORMANCE GUIDANCE
--------------------------------
In production, the IRIS dataset contains approximately:
  - 300,000+ rows per daily snapshot
  - 5,000+ active counterparties
  - Multiple products per counterparty per legal entity

ALWAYS filter by Date first when querying. Failing to filter by date will cause full-table scans
across multiple snapshots and will be extremely slow and memory-intensive.


COUNTRY RATINGS
---------------
Country ratings follow S&P-style notation:
  AAA — Top tier (e.g., CANADA)
  AA  — High quality (e.g., USA)
  A   — Upper medium grade (e.g., GERMANY)
  BBB — Lower medium grade (e.g., SWITZERLAND)
  BB  — Speculative (e.g., CAYMAN ISLANDS)
  B and below — Highly speculative / distressed


INTERNAL RATINGS
----------------
Customer Internal Rating is an integer from 1 (lowest quality / most risk) to 10 (highest quality
/ lowest risk). Counterparties with rating <= 4 require enhanced monitoring and closer oversight.

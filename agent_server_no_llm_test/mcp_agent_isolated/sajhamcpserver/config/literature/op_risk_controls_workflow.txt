Op Risk Controls Workflow — Agent Knowledge Base

What this workflow does:
Analyses a control description in 3 sequential LLM steps:
Step 1: 5Ws Analysis — decompose control into Who/What/Where/When/Why
Step 2: Design Effectiveness — assess whether control design adequately addresses the risk
Step 3: Operational Effectiveness — assess whether control is executable and auditable in practice

When to invoke it:
- User asks to "analyse", "review", "assess", or "evaluate" a control description
- "Is this a good control?"
- "What are the gaps in this control?"
- "Review this control for audit readiness"

What it requires:
At minimum: the control description text.
Control ID, type, owner, and risk domain improve output quality but are optional.

5Ws definition:
- WHO: Who performs it, who is accountable, who is the recipient
- WHAT: What specific action is taken, what evidence is produced
- WHERE: What system, process, or location it operates in
- WHEN: Frequency or trigger (event-based vs time-based)
- WHY: What risk it mitigates, what the objective is

Design effectiveness:
Whether the control is structurally sound — right type, right coverage, produces evidence, has escalation path.

Operational effectiveness:
Whether the control can realistically be executed — ownership clarity, frequency feasibility, auditable evidence.

Output format:
Step 1 output feeds into Step 2. Steps 1+2 feed into Step 3.
Final output is a complete analysis with overall rating (EFFECTIVE / PARTIALLY EFFECTIVE / INEFFECTIVE) and prioritised remediation plan (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW).

What it does NOT do:
Does not access any internal systems or external data — operates entirely on the text provided.
